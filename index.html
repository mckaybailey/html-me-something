I don't know much about what to say about myself, so im going to give you my three biggest hobbies.

  • Music
  • School
  • Computers

Music

Music was probably the most fun hobby that I've had, but also the least lucritive. It is not easy to make a lot of money doing music, but you can learn a lot from mastering the technique neccesary to play an instrument. The investment I made in music mostly paid off because it was a satisfying way to learn something difficult. I've done it since the fourth grade and learned how to play piano, tuba, guitar, bass, and ukelele.

School

I've always been naturally good at the random stuff they had me do at school even though I was not particularly interested in all of it. Eventually, I got used to not needing to really try on my homework, but then I got to the more confusing classes and realized that even if you are smart, anything worth its time is going to take time to learn. I realized this once it was too late. For my last two years of highschool, I went to a local community college and my calculus teacher did not assign homework. I thought I was smart enough to just not do it, but then I would not have time to do all the problems that I did not practice. I learned my lesson with a C, but I was still happy to pass the class unlike the other half of students who ended up dropping.

Computers

I feel like a lot of older people have an incoherent understanding of computers. I'm not saying that older people are bad with them, just more seperated. Growing up, I was surrounded by computers. Consequently, I was more likely to understand them and get good at using them. When I was little, I felt special when people would ask me for the wifi password because I had the random bunch of numbers memorized.
